# Merchant Credit (Working Capital) — Volume-Based

## What it is
A bank-provided working capital facility for eligible merchants, where eligibility and limits are informed by the merchant’s recent payment volume and stability.

## Merchant value
- Smooth cashflow during seasonal spikes
- Fund inventory / payroll / rent without waiting for settlements
- Faster access than traditional collateral-heavy lending (where eligible)

## Typical structures (bank-owned product)
- **Revolving working capital line** (limit reviewed periodically)
- **Short-tenor working capital loan** for peak periods

## Eligibility signals (high level)
- Consistent recent payment volume (GMV) and stable throughput
- Healthy success rate (low technical failure rate)
- Low disputes/chargebacks and controlled refund patterns
- Satisfactory KYC and risk profile per bank policy

## What the bank needs (demo-friendly)
- Last 30/60/90 day payment volume and success metrics
- Basic business details and bank account linkage
- KYC status and any risk flags

## How it works (conceptual)
- The bank uses your payment history to size an indicative limit.
- Repayments follow a standard lending schedule or can be aligned to cashflow.

## When to recommend
- Merchant asks about cashflow, payout timing, or funding operations
- Merchant is growing quickly and wants predictable working capital

## Caveats
- Final eligibility, pricing, and limits are subject to bank underwriting and policy.
- Not available for all merchant categories.
